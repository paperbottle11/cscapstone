Overview

Turtles are reptiles of the order Testudines. They are characterized by a special bony or cartilaginous shell developed from their ribs that acts as a shield. Turtles are found on every continent except for Antarctica.

Types

  • Green Turtle
  • Loggerhead Turtle
  • Hawksbill Turtle
  • Leatherback Turtle

Habitat

Turtles can be found in various habitats including oceans, rivers, lakes, and even deserts. They are adapted to live in diverse environments and have different preferences for temperature, water conditions, and food sources.

Behavior

Turtles are known for their slow and steady movements. They are predominantly herbivores but some species also feed on insects, small fish, and crustaceans. Turtles communicate through various methods including visual cues, touch, and vocalizations.

Conservation

Many turtle species are endangered or critically endangered due to habitat loss, pollution, climate change, and illegal trade. Conservation efforts are focused on protecting their nesting grounds and implementing measures to reduce threats to their survival.
