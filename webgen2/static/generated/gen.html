Capybara

The capybara is the largest rodent in the world. It is native to South America and can be found in savannas and dense forests near bodies of water. Capybaras are highly social animals and live in groups called 'cavies'. They are herbivorous and mainly feed on grasses and aquatic plants.

Capybaras have long, barrel-shaped bodies and short legs. They have webbed feet, which makes them excellent swimmers. Their fur is coarse and usually brown or gray in color. Capybaras have large, round heads with eyes and ears positioned on the top. They have blunt snouts and prominent teeth.

Capybaras are known for their relaxed and friendly nature. They are often seen resting or soaking in water to cool off during hot days. Capybaras communicate through vocalizations, scent marking, and body language. They have a lifespan of around 8-10 years in the wild.
