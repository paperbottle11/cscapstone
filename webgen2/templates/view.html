Welcome to Pickleball

Learn how to play pickleball and enjoy the game.

Rules

Pickleball is played on a badminton-sized court with the same net height as tennis. The rules are somewhat similar to tennis and ping pong.

Here are some key rules of pickleball:

  1. Each team must hit the ball over the net and into the opponent's court, starting with a serve.
  2. The ball must bounce once on each side before players can start volleying.
  3. The player who serves must do so underhand.
  4. If the ball lands on the line, it is considered as in.
  5. Games are typically played up to 11 points, but can also be up to 15 or 21 points depending on the players' preference.

Equipment

To play pickleball, you will need the following equipment:

  • Pickleball paddle
  • Pickleball
  • Pickleball court

Pickleball paddles are solid, hard-surface paddles made from various materials such as wood, graphite, or composite materials.

Techniques

Here are some basic techniques to master in pickleball:

  1. Serve: Start the game with an underhand serve, ensuring the ball clears the net and lands in the opponent's service court.
  2. Volley: Volley the ball before it bounces, hitting it back and forth over the net.
  3. Dink: Use a soft shot close to the net to strategically place the ball in difficult positions for the opponent.
  4. Smash: Execute a powerful overhead shot to drive the ball downward with force.
